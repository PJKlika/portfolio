About Me

Early-career developer with a U.S. Air Force background

Who I Am

I'm an early-career developer transitioning from a hands-on military communications role into modern web development. My experience comes from college projects at UMGC and self-directed builds—shipping responsive UIs, React apps, and XR prototypes that focus on clarity, performance, and real user outcomes.

My Air Force background (Radio Frequency / SATCOM & Cyber) taught me disciplined problem-solving, systems thinking, and reliability under pressure. I bring that same mindset to software: plan carefully, communicate clearly, execute consistently.

Career Goals

My career goal is to establish myself as a full-stack software engineer who bridges technical excellence with practical business impact. I aim to work in organizations that leverage current and emerging technologies—including cloud computing, artificial intelligence, augmented and virtual reality, and progressive web applications—to solve real-world problems in the digital media and computing industry.

I'm particularly drawn to roles where I can contribute to immersive digital experiences, whether that's building scalable web applications, developing AR/VR solutions for training and education, or creating interactive media that enhances how people learn and work. As the industry evolves toward spatial computing, AI-assisted development, and edge computing, I want to be at the forefront—continuously learning new frameworks, adapting to emerging best practices, and applying both traditional software engineering principles and innovative approaches to deliver user-centered solutions.

Long-term, I aspire to take on technical leadership roles where I can mentor junior developers, architect robust systems, and drive projects that balance cutting-edge technology with maintainability and accessibility. I believe the future of digital media lies in seamless integration of web, mobile, and extended reality platforms, and I'm committed to developing the cross-functional expertise needed to excel in this evolving landscape.

My Approach
